Ecosystem

An ecosystem is a community of living organisms and their physical environment that interact with each other:

  1. Living components: Plants, animals, fungi, and microorganisms
  2. Non-living components: Sunlight, water, air, temperature, oxygen, and soil
  3. Energy flow: Energy flows through the system, usually from light to heat
  4. Matter recycling: Matter is recycled within the ecosystem
  5. Ecosystems can be natural or artificial, and can be found on land or in water. Examples of artificial ecosystems include gardens, parks, aquariums, and croplands.
  6. The word ecosystem comes from the Greek words oikos, meaning "home," and systema, meaning "system". The term was proposed by British botanist Arthur Tansley.
Here are some other things to know about ecosystems:
  • Ecosystems can be of different sizes. Large ecosystems are often called biomes.
  • Ecosystems with higher biodiversity are more stable and resilient to disturbances.
  • Introducing new species or environmental factors can lead to the collapse of an ecosystem.
  • The term "ecosystem" has been used in many contexts outside of the natural environment, such as "app ecosystem" and "education ecosystem".
